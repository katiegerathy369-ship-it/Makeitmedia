The brief

An outdated site that didn't reflect her expertise

Amy had been practising naturopathy for years but her online presence didn't match the quality of her work. Her existing site was built on an outdated platform — slow, generic, and not converting visitors into bookings.

She needed something that felt premium, personal, and professional — a site that immediately communicated her expertise and made it easy for patients to book. And she needed someone who understood the naturopathic world well enough to write the copy without her having to explain it.

That's where Make It Media came in.

⚡
The problem
Outdated site on an old platform. Slow load times, no booking integration, copy that didn't reflect her clinical expertise, and no SEO foundations in place.
✦
The solution
A fully custom Next.js site with AI-assisted copywriting, Simple Clinic integration for online bookings, on-page SEO, GA4 analytics, and a brand identity that finally matched her level of practice.

What was delivered

Every piece of the puzzle

01
Custom website build
6-page site built on Next.js 14 with Tailwind CSS and Framer Motion animations. No WordPress, no templates, no shared aesthetic with any other site.
02
Brand new visual identity
Colour palette, typography, and visual language developed from scratch to reflect Amy's clinical expertise and warm, approachable practice style.
03
AI-assisted copywriting
Every word on the site written by Make It Media — using AI tools and naturopathic clinical background to write AHPRA-aware copy that converts.
04
Simple Clinic integration
Online booking linked directly to Amy's Simple Clinic account. Patients can book, complete intake forms, and manage appointments without Amy's manual involvement.
05
SEO foundations
On-page SEO across all pages — meta titles, descriptions, semantic HTML structure, image alt text, and Google Search Console setup and verification.
06
GA4 + analytics setup
Google Analytics 4 and Microsoft Clarity installed and verified. Amy can see exactly where patients come from, which pages they visit, and where they drop off.
