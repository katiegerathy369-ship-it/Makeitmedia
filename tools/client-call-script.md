# Client Call Script — Dot Points

**Goal:** Understand what she needs, show her you're the right person, close with a clear yes.
**Time:** ~45 minutes

---

## 1. Open — Rapport (5 min)

- "Thanks for jumping on — I've had a look at the sites you sent through and I've got some thoughts, but first I'd love to hear a bit more about your business."
- Let her talk. Don't pitch yet.
- Ask:
  - "Tell me about what you do and who you work with"
  - "How long have you been running the business?"
  - "What's your main way of getting clients right now?"

---

## 2. The Problem — Why She Needs This (10 min)

- "What made you start looking for a website now — was there a specific moment?"
- "Do you have anything online at the moment? What's frustrating about it?"
- "When someone finds you online, what do you want them to do?"
  - Book a call?
  - Fill out a form?
  - Just learn about you?
- "Who's your ideal client — if you could clone your best customer, who would that be?"
- "Is there anything you definitely don't want on the site?"

**Listen for:** pain points, urgency, budget signals, how tech-savvy she is

---

## 3. Reference Site Walkthrough (15 min)

- "Let's look at those 3 sites you sent me — I'll share my screen"
- For each site, ask:
  - **"What specifically drew you to this one?"**
  - "Is it the colours? The layout? The overall feeling?"
  - "Is there anything on this site you wouldn't want for yours?"
- After all 3:
  - "So the common thread I'm hearing is [X] — does that sound right?"
  - "If your ideal client landed on your site, what should they feel?"

**Mirror her language back.** If she says "clean and fresh" — say "clean and fresh," not "minimalist with intentional whitespace."

**Share what you noticed:**
- "I pulled the colour palette from this one — it's using [describe]. I think something in that direction would work well for you."
- "The typography on this one is really doing the heavy lifting — that's something I can absolutely recreate."

---

## 4. Show Your Work (5 min)

- "Let me show you something I built recently"
- Pull up Amy Angus or your strongest portfolio piece
- Keep it brief — 60 seconds max:
  - "This was a [type of business] in [location]"
  - "I built it in [X weeks] — custom design, no templates"
  - "She's now getting [bookings/enquiries] directly through the site"
- If relevant: "I also have a health science background, so I understand [her industry] from the inside"

---

## 5. What She Already Has (5 min)

- "Let's quickly go through what you've already got so I know where we're starting from"
- Run through:
  - "Do you have a logo?"
  - "Any brand colours or a style guide?"
  - "Professional photos — headshots, workspace, products?"
  - "Written copy or descriptions of your services, even in dot points?"
  - "Do you own a domain name?"
- If she doesn't have much: **"That's completely normal — most of my clients come to me at this stage. I'll handle the colour palette, fonts, and layout. You just need to send me photos and rough dot points about your services."**

---

## 6. Recommend + Price + Close (10 min)

### Recommend ONE package
Don't give her three options. Pick the right one based on what she's told you.

**If she just needs a website:**
- "Based on what you've described, the Presence package is the right fit."
- "That's a custom 6-page website — I write the copy, set up your booking integration, SEO foundations, analytics — the whole thing."
- "It's $2,200 plus GST."

**If she needs website + marketing launch:**
- "I'd recommend the Visibility package for you."
- "You get everything in Presence, plus a 60-minute brand session, a full 30-day Instagram content calendar with 12 branded graphics, and an email welcome sequence."
- "It's $3,200 plus GST."

### Payment
- "You can pay upfront, or I can split it into 4 weekly instalments — no interest, no credit check."

### If she qualifies for new business pricing:
- "I also have a new business rate — 20% off — for businesses launched in the last 12 months. I'd just need to verify that, but it would bring it down to [discounted price]."

### Timeline
- "Once I have your logo, photos, and service descriptions, I'll have the first draft to you within [7-10 days]."
- "You get 2 rounds of revisions from there."
- "Most sites go live within 2-3 weeks total."

### Close
- **Ask directly.** Don't trail off.
- "Does that sound like what you're looking for?"
- Wait for response.
- If yes: "Great — I'll send you the invoice and a checklist of what I need from you straight after this call."
- If she needs to think: "Of course. I'll send you a summary of everything we discussed so you have it in writing. When do you think you'll know by?"

---

## 7. If She Hesitates

**On price:**
- Don't discount immediately. Ask: "What's making you pause — is it the price itself, or are you unsure about the value?"
- If genuinely too expensive: offer the 4-week instalment plan
- If new business: offer the 20% new business rate
- Never go below your rate without removing scope

**On timing:**
- "I completely understand. I'll send everything through so you have it — no pressure at all."
- "I do have limited spots at founding rates, so if timing is a factor, it's worth locking in sooner rather than later."

**On "I need to talk to my partner/husband/business partner":**
- "Absolutely — would it help if I put together a one-page summary you can show them?"

---

## 8. End the Call

- "Thanks so much for your time — I'm really excited about this"
- Confirm next step: "So I'll send you [invoice / summary / checklist] by [time today]"
- "Any questions at all, just reply to the email"

---

## Post-Call Checklist

- [ ] Send follow-up email within 1 hour (while she's still excited)
- [ ] Attach invoice
- [ ] Include brand asset checklist
- [ ] Add call notes to onboarding template
- [ ] Set calendar reminder: follow up in 48 hours if no response
- [ ] Extract hex codes + fonts from her reference sites
- [ ] Start wireframe/mood board if she said yes
