## Site 1: Lesley O'Connor Naturopath
**URL:** https://lesleyoconnornaturopath.com.au/
**Platform:** WordPress + Elementor + WooCommerce
**Note:** Client mentioned it only loads on mobile, not desktop — worth mentioning this is a build quality issue, not a design choice.

### What works
- **Warm, feminine colour palette** — dusty rose (#c47c84) with terracotta hover (#d56544). Earthy, wellness-appropriate tones that feel inviting without being clinical
- **Strong positioning immediately** — "Fertility Naturopath" is clear and specific. No ambiguity about what she does
- **Professional photography front and centre** — practitioner headshot as the hero. Builds trust instantly
- **E-commerce integration** — sells supplements/products alongside consultations. Dual revenue model

### What doesn't work
- **Broken on desktop** — if the site doesn't load on desktop, that's a fundamental build problem. WordPress + Elementor sites are notorious for this
- **Likely slow** — WordPress + Elementor + WooCommerce + MailerLite popup = plugin bloat
- **Popup email capture** — MailerLite popup is intrusive, especially on a first visit

### Colour palette to extract
| Swatch | Hex | Usage |
|--------|-----|-------|
| Dusty rose | #c47c84 | Primary accent, buttons |
| Terracotta | #d56544 | Hover states |
| White | #ffffff | Background |
| Dark text | #333333 | Body copy (assumed) |

### What to say on the call
"This one has a really warm, approachable feel — the dusty rose palette works well for women's health. The headshot-as-hero approach builds trust immediately. I can definitely create that warmth. The fact that it's broken on desktop is actually a good example of why I build custom — no plugin conflicts, no Elementor bloat."

## Site 2: Dr Leanne Chapman
**URL:** https://www.drleannechapman.com.au/
**Platform:** Squarespace

### What works
- **Clinical credibility done right** — MBBS FRANZCOG credentials in the hero, hospital affiliations visible, professional body logos in footer. Establishes authority without being cold
- **Clean, minimal layout** — lots of whitespace, clear typography hierarchy, nothing competing for attention
- **Strong messaging** — "Personalised care of the highest quality, delivered with kindness, sensitivity and respect." One sentence that says everything
- **Clear navigation structure** — About, Obstetrics, Gynaecology, Patient Hub, Refer, Contact. Logical, no clutter
- **Professional photography** — high-quality portraits in clinical settings. Warm but credible
- **Subtle animations** — fade-in transitions (0.6s delay, 0.4s duration). Smooth without being distracting
- **Phone number in header** — direct, low-friction contact

### What doesn't work
- **Squarespace limitations** — templated, limited customisation. The design is good but constrained
- **Generic sans-serif typography** — system fonts, nothing distinctive
- **No booking integration visible** — phone/email only, no online booking

### Colour palette to extract
| Swatch | Hex | Usage |
|--------|-----|-------|
| White | #ffffff | Background |
| Off-white/cream | (light neutral) | Section backgrounds |
| Dark charcoal | #2a2a2a (approx) | Text |
| Muted accent | (subtle blue or grey) | Links, CTAs |

### What to say on the call
"This is the most polished of the three. What stands out is the restraint — it's not trying to do too much. The credentials are visible without being showy, and the messaging is really clear. If you like this clean, professional feel, I can build on that aesthetic but with better typography and a warmer colour palette."

## Site 3: Seed & Flourish
**URL:** https://www.seedandflourish.com/
**Platform:** Wix

### What works (based on structure)
- **The name itself** — "Seed & Flourish" has the same growth/botanical energy as your brand. Your client may be drawn to this natural, organic feel
- **Modern design system** — CSS custom properties, responsive grid, smooth transitions
- **Animation on scroll** — opacity/transform effects triggered by scrolling. Adds polish
- **View transitions** — page-to-page transitions (slide, fade). Makes it feel like an app, not a website
- **Clean grid layout** — structured but flexible, supports complex arrangements

### What doesn't work
- **Wix platform** — slow, bloated JavaScript, poor SEO out of the box
- **Heavy framework** — Wix's Thunderbolt renderer adds significant overhead
- **Limited content visibility** — the Wix framework makes it hard to even crawl the actual content, which is bad for Google too

### What to say on the call
"I couldn't fully crawl this one because of how Wix builds its pages — which is actually a problem in itself, because Google has the same issue. But the design direction is clear — it has a natural, botanical feel with smooth animations. That's very achievable with a custom build, and it'll load three times faster."

## Common Threads Across All Three

### What she's drawn to (likely)
1. **Warm, feminine, wellness-appropriate colours** — dusty rose, sage, cream, earth tones. Not corporate blue, not clinical white
2. **Professional photography as the centrepiece** — all three lead with practitioner photos. She wants to be visible and build personal trust
3. **Clean layouts with breathing room** — whitespace, clear hierarchy, nothing cluttered
4. **Credibility signals** — qualifications, affiliations, specific positioning (not generic "naturopath" but "fertility naturopath")
5. **Botanical/natural aesthetic** — two of three sites have organic naming or earth-toned palettes

### What to avoid
- Plugin bloat (Site 1's desktop failure)
- Generic template feel (Site 2's Squarespace constraints)
- Slow load times from platform overhead (Site 3's Wix bloat)

### Design direction to propose
- **Palette:** Warm neutrals + a dusty rose or sage accent (pull from Site 1's dusty rose or go earthy/botanical like Site 3's name suggests)
- **Typography:** Serif display headings (like your Cormorant Garamond) + clean sans-serif body. This will immediately elevate it above all three reference sites
- **Photography:** She needs professional headshots — make sure to ask if she has these or needs guidance
- **Layout:** Clean hero with her photo, clear positioning statement, credentials visible, then services, then booking CTA
- **Animations:** Subtle fade-in on scroll (like Site 2), nothing flashy
